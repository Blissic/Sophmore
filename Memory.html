Memory

RAM is your PC's quick-access workspace. It holds all the data your system is actively using—like open apps, files, and game levels—so your CPU can grab it instantly. The more RAM you have, the more tasks your PC can juggle smoothly without slowing down. But unlike storage (your "long-term memory"), RAM forgets everything when you power off. Think of it like a desk: bigger desk (more RAM) = more projects you can work on at once!
